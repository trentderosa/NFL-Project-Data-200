% ---------------- SLIDE 1 ----------------
\begin{frame}{Project Goal}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Our project focused on predicting how NFL teams may perform in 2026.
    \item We used last season performance data along with draft-related data.
    \item The main goal was to see which factors had the strongest impact on predicted wins.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

% ---------------- SLIDE 2 ----------------
\begin{frame}{Predicted 2026 Wins by Team}
\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[width=0.9\textwidth]{predicted_wins_chart.png}
\end{center}
\end{frame}

% ---------------- SLIDE 3 ----------------
\begin{frame}{Talking Points: Predicted Wins}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The model predicts teams like KC, PHI, and BAL to perform near the top.
    \item Lower-performing teams from last season are still projected near the bottom.
    \item This gives a clear picture of expected team strength going into 2026.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

% ---------------- SLIDE 4 ----------------
\begin{frame}{Draft Score vs Predicted Wins}
\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[width=0.9\textwidth]{draft_vs_wins.png}
\end{center}
\end{frame}

% ---------------- SLIDE 5 ----------------
\begin{frame}{Talking Points: Draft Score}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The relationship between draft score and predicted wins is very weak.
    \item The correlation value is only r = 0.08.
    \item This suggests draft results alone are not enough to predict team success.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

% ---------------- SLIDE 6 ----------------
\begin{frame}{2025 Wins vs Predicted 2026 Wins}
\begin{center}
    \includegraphics[width=0.9\textwidth]{wins_comparison.png}
\end{center}
\end{frame}

% ---------------- SLIDE 7 ----------------
\begin{frame}{Talking Points: Past Performance}
\begin{itemize}
    \item This plot shows a very strong relationship between 2025 wins and predicted 2026 wins.
    \item The correlation value is r = 0.99.
    \item Based on our model, past team performance was the strongest predictor.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

% ---------------- SLIDE 8 ----------------
\begin{frame}{Main Takeaway}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Our biggest takeaway was that draft results matter, but they do not tell the full story.
    \item Teams that performed well last season are more likely to be projected higher.
    \item A better prediction comes from combining draft data with team performance data.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

% ---------------- FINAL ----------------
\begin{frame}{What We Learned}
\begin{itemize}
    \item We learned how to use data visualizations to explain a real sports prediction problem.
    \item We also learned that one variable by itself usually is not enough to explain team success.
    \item This project showed us how Python can turn raw data into useful talking points.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
